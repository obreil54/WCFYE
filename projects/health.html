Health

WCFYE puts the health sector as a top priority and believes that the betterment of the quality of life for Yemenis starts when the health sector runs effectively to serve the population as it should.

Yemen's health sector remains almost non-existent since the start of the civil war in 2014. Roughly half of Yemen's health facilities have closed their doors due to lack of operational support and resouces. The remaining hospitals are operating at minimal capacity and are in need of support.

Meanwhile, women and children have tremendously suffered due to medical shortages in public hospitals and clinics. Without adequate healthcare, citizens are at imminent risk of illness.

Our Support To The Health Sector:

Dozens of medical supplies distributed to rural hospitals.

Granting wheelchairs to hundreds of disabled people, with a specific focus on children.

Providing medicine to thousands of beneficiaries at rural hospitals.
